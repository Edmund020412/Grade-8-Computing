Our chapter of the Student regulation is:

Chapter 3: STUDENT RIGHTS AND OBLIGATIONS

Student Rights

Student rights are the legal and ethical entitlements students have within an educational institution, encompassing freedoms like speech and association, as well as rights to due process, safety, privacy, and equal access to education. These rights ensure fair treatment and protect students from discrimination while allowing them to engage in their education fully and receive high-quality instruction and support.

  1. Participate in academic and non-academic activities conducted both inside and outside the school, including online and onsite formats.
  2. Receive religious education in accordance with Christian values.
  3. Utilize all school facilities appropriately,according to their intended functions and regulations.
  4. Receive equal treatment from the school principal,teachers, and staff.
  5. Access guidance and counseling services from counselors or teachers based on needs and procedures.
  6. Receive health services provided by the school clinic in accordance with needs and regulations.
  7. Develop themselves by participating in Co-Curricular Activities(CCA) provided by the school.
  8. Take remedial or make-up assessments as approved by subject teachers based on needs and guidelines.
  9. Receive feedback or results on assignments or assessments within two weeks at the latest.
  10. Submit constructive suggestions or feedback to the school leadership, teachers, or staff in accordance with proper procedures and ethics.
  11. Participate in competitions or events on behalf of the school with the principal's approval.
  12. Receive information and take part in selection processesfor self-development opportunities (scholarships, competitions, student exchange programs).
  13. Receive report slips or diplomas regularly, provided all administrative payments and returns of borrowed school property have been fulfilled.
  14. Receive report card or diplomas upon withdrawal or graduation, provided that all administrative obligations and the return of borrowed property have been completed.

Student Obligations

Student obligations include academic responsibilities like attending classes, completing homework, and striving to do their best, as well as behavioral and social duties such as respecting teachers and peers, obeying school rules, and contributing to a positive learning environment.

  1. Understand and uphold the school's core values: Love, Joy, Peace, Patience, Kindness, Goodness, Faithfulness, Gentleness, and Self-Control.
  2. Comply with allschool rules, both written and verbal.
  3. Show respect to all members of the school community through polite behavior and speech.
  4. Maintain the good reputation of the school both on and off campus, including on social media.
  5. Be present atschool on time during in-person learning.
  6. Participate in all classes and school programs.
  7. Inform and request permission when absent;five consecutive unexcused absences are considered a withdrawal.
  8. Ask for clarification or updates on lessons or school activities that were missed or not understood.
  9. Come prepared with books and learning tools as required.
  10. Actively participate in school events and activities.
  11. Maintain cleanliness, a comfortable environment, and classroom order.
  12. Be responsible forschool property and replace any damage caused by negligence or intent.
  13. Take care of personal belongings at school (mobile phones must be submitted to the homeroom teacher).
  14. Dress neatly and modestly according to the following standards:
    1. Monday until Thursday : National Uniform (white-Blue)
      1. Male Student:
        • Shirtbuttoned and tucked into trousers
        • School tie
        • School cap during flag ceremony
        • Belt
        • School attributes (logo and name tag)
      2. Female Students:
        • Blouse/shirt buttoned and tucked into skirt
        • Skirt below the knee
        • School cap during flag ceremony
        • School tie
        • School attributes (logo and name tag)
    2. Friday - Batik attire for both male and female students
      • Collared shirt
      • Fabric pants or jeans, notleggings, gympants, or ripped jeans
    3. PE Day - School sports uniform
      • Must change into regular uniformafter PE class.
      • If PE is in the last period,students must wear their regular uniform first and change into PE clothes only when PE starts.
      • May wear PE uniform from home if PE is the first period
    4. Other activities - Smart casual attire
      • Top must have sleeves and not be see-through
      • Fabric pants or jeans (not leggings, gympants, or ripped jeans)
      • Any color shoes with socks must be worn
      • It is mandatory for students to wear shoes that adhere to school regulations; sandals are strictly prohibited within the school environment. Sandals may only be worn by students who are genuinely unwell and present a doctor's note or a letter from their parents
  15. Follow grooming standards
    1. Male Student:
      • Short, neat haircut (front: eyebrow line,side: ear line, back: collarline)
      • No hair coloring
      • Appropriate accessories
      • No shaved designs or patterns
    2. Female Students:
      • Neat hairstyle
      • No hair coloring
      • No makeup except for powder and colorlesslip gloss
      • Appropriate accessories
      • No nail polish
  16. Resolve problems according to applicable procedures.
  17. Accept consequences and participate in guidance if a violation is committed.
  18. Pay school tuition according to the procedures and deadlines set by the school.
  19. Request permission before using school facilities outside ofschool hours or on holidays.
  20. Use social media and online platforms responsibly and in alignment with learning purposes, avoiding hate speech, provocation, or contentrelated to ethnicity, religion, race, or intergroup conflict.
